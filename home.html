The Clone by Alloramomo

When my clone first arrived, I was excited.

I wouldn't need to work or do chores.

It was strange at first, to watch yourself in third person.

I had to remind myself that he wasn't a person at all.

I tried not to talk to him, and referring to him as "it";

To remove the semblance of humanity.

And after a year I grew more accustomed.

Even began to see it's behavior differ from mine.

It made me laugh once, which was jarring.

I asked it.. "Do you want to be me?"

To which it replied, "I am nobody."

And thus I began assigning more responsibiltiy.

Call my girlfriend, check on my friends, and for the time it was good.

I'd rid myself of responsibility but I began to question;

What makes us human?

Was it our relationships? Our chores? Our thoughts?

Because as time passed, my relationships began to deteriorate.

"He's more you than you are."

I was unable to work.

And I couldn't put together thoughts because I'd harbored myself from my life.

I started looking at it the way it must've first viewed me.

As I became a lifeless machine, and he was living my life.

She loved him. They loved him.

I thought about killing him, but what was that going to do?

I didn't realise by assigning him my life, that I would be taking on his.

Be careful what you wish for.

... The End ...

Learn from our mistakes. Death is greed.
